Step 2: Definition
  1. Verb “To Be” in the English Language is a linking verb that tell us what the subject is. Not what the subject is doing and links the subject to the subject complement (adjective).

 

 

Step 3:Disappearance
  1. The Verb “To Be” as you see disappeared in the Arabic language! Why?
In the Arabic Language there are two types of sentences:
  • Nominal sentence begins with a noun.
  • Verbal sentence begins with a verb.

    •  

     

    4:Nominal Sentence 1st Rule
  • Since these two sentences begin with a noun , they are nominal sentences.
    • These two sentences are classified nominal sentences based only on the word rule . It has nothing to do with the omission of the verb to be.
  • Where did the Verb "To Be" go in the Present positive Form?
    • In the formal English Language context there is no room for verbless sentences and nouns are linked to adjectives using the Verb "To Be".
  • However, in the Arabic Language structure allows on certain conditions nominal sentences to form without needing any verb at all, and the Verb “To Be” form is no exception.
  • These conditions Verb "To Be" acting as a linking verb in the simple present and the positive form.Under these conditions, the verb will be omitted allowing the most basic form of nominal sentences in the Arabic language to emerge, which is a noun adjective sentence.

    •  

     

    Step 5:Nominal Sentence 2nd Rule
    1. This form of nominal sentences is identified when the noun and the adjective describing it failed to agree in terms of definiteness.

     

     

    Step 6:Definite and Indefinite Alliance
    1. If the >noun is definite and the adjective is indefinite and the alliance provides a complete message, then it is a complete sentence.
    2. As opposed to the English language which uses its linking Verb “To Be” in the present simple tense (positive form), the Arabic Language does not.
    3. In Modern Standard Arabic Language verbless sentences are allowed and they represent the most basic form of nominal sentences.
